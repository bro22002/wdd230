Alignment

UXPERT

uxpert.com

The logos are aligned horizontally and vertically pairs. They are horizontally aligned in composition making them visually appealing. This makes it simple and easy to understand. While you probably won't notice when the elements in a design are aligned, you will almost definitely notice when they aren't. A design with poor alignment will look cluttered and unfinished.

Alignment is the placement of visual elements so they line up in composition. In design, we use alignment to organize elements, group elements, create balance, create structure, create connections between elements, to create a sharp and clear outcome.

White Space

Ensign College

ensign.edu

Ensign College has good use of white space around the elements of the page. This allows users to breathe and stay focused by reducing the amount of text and functional elements users see at once. The effective use of white space on the page has led to a clean and appealing design.

Whitespace not only creates harmony, and balance, and helps to brand design, it can also be used to lead a reader from one element to another. The main goals are to make the website look simple and uncluttered and to deliver information that readers will enjoy and appreciate.

Contrast

Airbnb

airbnb.com

In the screenshot above, we can see that contrast has been applied to visual elements in a different composition. This has increased impact and creates exciting graphics and visual interest in the context of the page. The choice of colors on the page has created a balance, rhythm, and pattern with the other elements of the page. The differences make various elements stand out and draw viewers' attention to the content of the page.

Contrast. As a principle of art, contrast refers to the arrangement of opposite elements and effects. For example, light and dark colors, smooth and rough textures, and large and small shapes. Contrast can be used to create variety, visual interest, and drama in an artwork.
